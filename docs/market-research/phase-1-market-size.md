# Market Research: Phase 1 — Market Size & Viability

**Date:** April 9, 2026  
**Research Method:** YouTube search analysis, channel profile inspection, pattern recognition  
**Focus:** Live poker vloggers (physical table recording, NOT online-only streamers)

---

## Executive Summary

The live poker vlogging niche on YouTube represents a **clearly defined, actively growing market** with approximately **15-25 active creators** producing regular long-form content. The market spans from hobbyists (1K-10K subs) to established pros (700K+ subs), with identifiable "paying customer" profiles who demonstrate serious investment signals (sponsorships, Twitch cross-posting, personal websites).

**Key Finding:** This is NOT a saturated market. The barrier to entry (consistent recording, editing quality, upload discipline) creates natural filtering, and the most successful channels show remarkable upload consistency and professional production values.

## 7. Viability Assessment

### ✅ STRONG INDICATORS

1. **Clear paying customer profile**
   - Channels with sponsorships, merch, Twitch, personal websites demonstrate willingness to invest in their brand
   - 6-8 channels already have business infrastructure (websites, monetization)

2. **Market is not saturated**
   - Only ~18 active live poker vloggers identified
   - Clear differentiation between "pro" and "hobbyist" tiers
   - Entry barriers (editing quality, upload consistency) prevent flooding

3. **Growing production values**
   - Channels investing in 4K, professional editing, chapters
   - Indicates budget available for premium tools

4. **Sustainable content model**
   - Episode-numbered series demonstrate long-term commitment
   - Weekly upload schedules show business-like discipline

5. **Platform alignment**
   - YouTube rewards long-form content (ideal for vlogs)
   - Channel memberships, sponsorships, affiliate programs all monetizable

### ⚠️ RISKS

1. **Niche is small**
   - 15-20 paying customers max in near term
   - Need to either expand TAM (ASR for broader poker content) or find adjacent markets

2. **Competition from free tools**
   - DaVinci Resolve, CapCut offer free editing
   - Must differentiate on specialized poker features (card recognition, HUD overlay)

3. **Player turnover**
   - Poker players fluctuate in success (variance)
   - Losing streaks could reduce content quality/quantity

4. **Platform risk**
   - YouTube algorithm changes could impact discovery
   - Diversification to Twitch/TikTok increasingly common (Jeff Boski)

### 💡 OPPORTUNITIES

1. **ASR/Transcription Feature**
   - Extract player commentary automatically (mentioned in issue context)
   - Could become "poker vlog standard" feature
   - Expands TAM to broader poker content creators

2. **Card Recognition AI**
   - Automatically overlay hole cards, community cards
   - High-value differentiator for poker-specific editing

3. **Community Expansion**
   - Poker vloggers collaborate (channel features, tournament crossovers)
   - Community-building platform could become sticky

4. **Training/Strategy Integration**
   - Jonathan Little model: combine vlog with educational content
   - Could create upsell path to training products

## 9. Conclusion

**The live poker vlogging market is VIABLE but SMALL.**

- **Strengths:** Clear paying customers, sustainable business models, growing production values
- **Weaknesses:** Small TAM (15-20 paying customers), niche focus
- **Verdict:** Worth pursuing **IF**:
  1. MVP can be built quickly (≤2 months)
  2. Price point can reach $50-100/mo (justified by poker-specific features)
  3. Expansion path exists (ASR → broader poker content market)
